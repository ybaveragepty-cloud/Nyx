# Eastern Cape Aquaponics Farm

**Source:** 3 ChatGPT conversations (~9,607 lines total)
**Date:** ~2026-03-10
**GPT:** Eastern Cape Aquaponics Farm (separate project from YBAFlow and La Buschagne)

## Summary

A separate consulting/equity project — an existing but underperforming aquaponics facility in the Eastern Cape being assessed and rebuilt into commercial-scale Tilapia and Pangasius/Basa production. Richard uses ChatGPT as a consulting tool for diagnostics, upgrade design, SOP creation, and equipment specification.

## Facility Assessment

- **Location:** Eastern Cape, South Africa (colder climate)
- **Existing:** 15 rectangular cement-block tanks (~2.5 m³ each), above-ground, gravity-fed recirculation
- **Problems:** No heating (tilapia need ~28°C), no photoperiod control, no incubation, insufficient flow (25 m³/hr, needs ≥37)
- **Potential with upgrades:** ~31,000 fingerlings/month

## Production Targets

- **1,000 kg Tilapia/month** to market
- Harvest weight: 600-800g, grow-out cycle: 10-11 months
- Stocking density: 60 kg/m³
- Species: Tilapia (primary) + Pangasius/Basa (secondary)

## Infrastructure Specs

### Nursery (8 × 2,500L = 20,000L)
- Target temp: 28°C, RAS design
- Flow: 32-36 m³/hr
- Heat pump: Alliance Smart Aqua R32 18kW Inverter (2 preferred)
- Pumps: 3 × SOBO BO-16000

### Grow-out (4 × 30,000L)
- Flow: 180 m³/hr
- Heating: ITS 35kW Inverter (70-90 kW total recommended)
- Pumps: 2 × Speroni Drainage 3.0kW/80mm/400V

### Hatchery
- 2 × 8L overflow incubation cups
- 2 × 20L fry holding tanks
- Auto temp control, Matala filtration
- Egg target: ~2,000 eggs/month per species (50-70% survival)

## Broodstock Plan
- **Tilapia:** 2 active breeding tanks (1M:3F per tank) + 1 reserve
- **Pangasius:** Induced spawning, external broodstock (2F + 2M + 1 spare)

## SOP Created

Full modular SOP covering: daily/weekly/monthly checklists, grading protocols, loss accounting, tank rotation framework, broodstock management for both species.
