## Summary

A professional grant proposal draft for YBA's commercial aquaponics operation, plus a comprehensive survey of all South African funding options (government, private, development, international).

## Grant Proposal Highlights

- **Project:** Scalable Commercial Aquaponics Production System
- **Location:** Western Cape, South Africa
- **Duration:** 18-24 months
- **System:** 5 × 13,400L tanks, DWC hydroponics, media beds, mineralization chambers

### Production Targets
- 625 fish introduced monthly, 625 harvested at 500g = 312.5kg/month per unit
- Year 1 scaled: 24 tons fish
- Daily leafy green + specialty fruiting crop production

### Technical Design
- Parallel pump redundancy (3-5 hour buffer)
- Central manifold air system with Matala diffusers
- 3 aerobic mineralization boxes with EM bacterial inoculation
- BRIX-focused plant health strategy (fulvic/humic, Trichoderma, Bacillus)
- Greenhouse: root zone 18-23°C, 60 air changes/hour

## South African Funding Landscape

### Government
- **DALRRD** — agriculture grants (slow, politically influenced)
- **dtic** — agro-processing support (R1M-R20M+, heavy compliance)
- **SEFA** — government-backed loans
- **Land Bank** — agricultural loans
- **IDC** — large development funding (R5M+, job creation required)
- **Western Cape Dept of Agriculture** — faster than national
- **Wesgro/GreenCape** — green economy investor matching

### Private
- Commercial bank loans
- Angel investors
- VC (Knife Capital, 4Di, HAVAÍC, Futuregrowth)
- **ESG/Impact investors** — strongest angle for aquaponics
- Corporate partnerships (Pick n Pay, Woolworths, Shoprite)

### International
- Green Climate Fund, GEF, African Development Bank
- Dutch/Norwegian aquaculture grants
- African Blue Economy funds

### Recommended for Richard
1. ESG/Impact investors (best fit)
2. IDC for scale
3. Provincial agriculture grants
4. Offtake-backed bank financing
