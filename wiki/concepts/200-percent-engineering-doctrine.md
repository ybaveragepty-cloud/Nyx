# 200% Engineering Doctrine

A La Buschagne institutional law: all critical systems must be engineered to double capacity. Operational biomass is capped at 50% of engineered capacity. This is not a guideline or a recommendation — it is a non-negotiable rule that defines the engineering culture of the entire operation.

## The Rule

Every critical system — biofiltration, aeration, water turnover, solids removal, electrical redundancy — must handle twice the load it will ever see in normal operations. The system is designed for the worst-case combined stress scenario, then operated at half that ceiling. This means:

- If you stock 1,000 kg of fish, your systems handle 2,000 kg
- If you need 37 m³/hr flow, you engineer for 74 m³/hr
- If you need one heat pump, you install two

The doctrine extends beyond aquaculture hardware. It applies to any system where failure means loss — biological, mechanical, or financial.

## Why 200%

> Operate at 80% of system capability. Operators who push 100% burn out or crash.

The aquaculture industry loses farms to system failures that were entirely predictable. A single pump failure, a biofilter crash during a temperature spike, an aeration dropout during peak feeding — any of these kills fish. The 200% doctrine eliminates the category of "we didn't have enough capacity" failures entirely. What remains are operator errors and genuinely unforeseeable events, both of which are smaller risk categories.

## Relationship to the Franchise Model

The [[franchise-operations-model]] enforces this doctrine across all network farms. Franchise partners cannot deviate from engineering standards without written HQ approval. This is one of the key differentiators between a La Buschagne franchise and an independent farm — the franchise guarantees a level of engineering discipline that most independent operators skip to save cost.

The doctrine is also a core component of the [[ip-tiering-strategy]] — the specific engineering calculations and redundancy specifications sit at Tier 3 (Internal Engineering Standards, HQ only).
