# IP Tiering Strategy

A three-tier intellectual property structure that governs what knowledge is shared, with whom, and under what conditions. This is how the La Buschagne franchise empire protects its competitive advantage while still operating an open training and franchise model.

## The Three Tiers

### Tier 1 — Public Knowledge
General aquaponics information available to anyone. This includes basic system design principles, species selection guidance, and introductory training materials. Publishing this content establishes La Buschagne as a thought leader and drives inbound interest from potential franchise partners and students.

### Tier 2 — Franchise Ops Manual (NDA Protected)
The La Buschagne Franchise Operations Manual — the complete playbook for running a franchise farm to La Buschagne standards. This includes SOPs, quality thresholds, equipment specifications, supplier lists, and operational procedures. Access requires signing an NDA and entering a formal franchise agreement.

This tier contains the operational knowledge that makes a La Buschagne farm perform above industry average. It is the core value proposition of the franchise — partners pay for access to proven, documented systems rather than figuring it out independently.

### Tier 3 — Internal Engineering Standards (HQ Only)
The deepest engineering knowledge: proprietary system designs, the specific calculations behind the [[200-percent-engineering-doctrine]], advanced breeding protocols, and the institutional knowledge that took years to develop. This tier never leaves HQ. Not even franchise partners see it.

Tier 3 is the moat. Even if a franchise partner leaves the network, they take Tier 2 knowledge (which they signed for) but never Tier 3. This ensures that La Buschagne always maintains a technical advantage over any former partner who might become a competitor.

## Why Three Tiers

A two-tier model (public vs. private) does not account for the franchise relationship. Franchise partners need more than public knowledge to operate effectively, but they do not need — and should not have — the deepest engineering IP. The three-tier model creates a middle ground: enough knowledge to operate excellently, not enough to replicate the engineering advantage independently.

## Application Beyond Aquaponics

This tiering model is applicable to the broader YBA ecosystem. [[ybaflow]]'s SaaS products could follow the same structure: public documentation (Tier 1), customer-specific configurations and playbooks (Tier 2), and internal platform engineering standards (Tier 3).
