First, the key biological difference
Tilapia can spawn repeatedly in a controlled hatchery. Female Nile tilapia can spawn continuously when there is no cold period, and egg output scales with female size; roughly 100 eggs from a 100 g female and 1,000–1,500 eggs from a 600–1,000 g female. Spawning in breeding populations tends to be asynchronous every 3–4 weeks. 

Pangasius/basa is different. It is generally run through induced breeding, not continuous natural tank spawning. A mature female can produce far more than 2,000 eggs per spawn—FAO notes a 10 kg female can produce over one million eggs—but females usually mature only after 3–4 years, and broodfish are typically managed separately from a small hatchery nursery. 

So the design logic is:

Tilapia: use hatchery tanks as true breeding tanks.

Pangasius: do not try to use the same 2.5 m³ tanks as permanent broodstock tanks for routine breeding. Use them only as a short-term induced spawning / egg handling / larval holding area. That is the technically sound route.
