I am a 26-year-old woman, soon to be 27, and the mother of a young daughter who is almost two years old. I am seeking protection because I fear for our safety and future in my country of nationality, South Africa.

I previously lived in KwaZulu-Natal and later relocated to Cape Town in the hope of finding better work opportunities and a safer environment for my family. Despite these efforts, I have been unable to achieve stability or security. I am currently residing on a farm in the Joostenberg Vlakte area, which is surrounded by communities with high levels of violent crime. These conditions pose a constant threat to our daily safety and well-being. As a young woman caring for a small child, I feel particularly vulnerable in an environment where violent crime is common and protection is limited.

I have personally experienced the consequences of this insecurity. My husband was physically attacked during an attempted hijacking and was stabbed during the incident. He narrowly survived. This violent attack had a lasting emotional and psychological impact on me and increased my fear for my family’s safety.

I also experienced a profound and personal loss due to the lack of reliable emergency and medical care. At the time, I was three months pregnant. My mother did not have medical aid and, without my knowledge, attempted to call for an ambulance in the days leading up to her death. Emergency services indicated that assistance was dispatched, but the ambulance never arrived. When her condition worsened, I personally took her to a government hospital. She required oxygen and urgent medical attention, but despite my efforts, she did not survive. Losing my mother during my pregnancy caused severe emotional distress and reinforced my fear that access to timely medical care cannot be relied upon, especially for vulnerable people.

My current living situation does not allow for personal or economic growth, and the farm business has not provided the security or stability that was hoped for. I am unable to create a safe or sustainable future for myself and my daughter under these circumstances. The ongoing insecurity and lack of viable alternatives cause constant fear and anxiety about what will happen to us if we remain.

I do not have immediate family support in South Africa, as my close family members live abroad and are unable to provide assistance or protection. This leaves me and my daughter without a support system or safe place to turn to in times of crisis.

Because of these circumstances — including direct exposure to violent crime, the traumatic loss of my mother during my pregnancy due to lack of timely medical care, and my ongoing vulnerability as a young mother — I fear that remaining in South Africa will continue to expose us to danger, instability, and harm. I am seeking resettlement to protect my child and myself and to provide her with the opportunity to grow up in a safe and secure environment.
