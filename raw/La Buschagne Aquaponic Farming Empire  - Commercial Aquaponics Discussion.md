## 1️⃣ Ammonia Testing in Commercial Systems

**Question:**  
Home systems test weekly.  
What is best practice in commercial systems and why?

### Correct Manager-Level Answer:

**Daily ammonia testing (minimum once per day).**

### Why?

1. **Fish biomass density is exponentially higher**
	- Small mistakes escalate fast.
		- Commercial systems operate at tight ammonia margins.
2. **Feed input is controlled daily**
	- Ammonia load is directly proportional to feed.
		- If feeding increases → ammonia risk increases within hours.
3. **Biofilter failure must be detected immediately**
	- Nitrosomonas die quickly if DO drops.
		- One pump failure = ammonia spike within hours.
		- Waiting a week = dead fish.

If you’re running trout at commercial density and miss one spike — you lose tens of thousands.

That’s not theory. That’s reality.

## 2️⃣ High Ammonia – Causes

When ammonia spikes, don’t panic. Diagnose.

Four major causes:

1. Overfeeding
2. Dead fish in tank
3. Biofilter under-capacity
4. Low dissolved oxygen in biofilter
5. pH crash affecting nitrifiers
6. Temperature drop slowing Nitrobacter
7. Sludge accumulation causing anaerobic zones

A junior manager reacts.  
A senior manager diagnoses the root cause.

---

## 3️⃣ Which Spikes First if Biofilter Damaged?

**Ammonia spikes first.**

Why?

Because:

- AOB (Nitrosomonas) convert ammonia → nitrite.
- If they fail, ammonia accumulates immediately.
- Nitrite only spikes if NOB (Nitrobacter) fail.

This shows understanding of nitrification sequencing.
